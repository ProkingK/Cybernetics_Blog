CYBERNETICS

The science of communications and automatic control systems in both machines and living things

What is Cybernetics ?

Cybernetics is the study of the control and communication systems in living organisms and machines. It is a field that combines engineering, biology, computer science, and other disciplines to understand how systems process information and make decisions.

At its core, cybernetics is concerned with understanding how systems function and how they can be controlled and maintained. This includes both natural and artificial systems, such as the human body and its various systems (e.g. circulatory, respiratory), as well as technological systems like computers and robots.

The goal of cybernetics is to understand how systems can be designed and optimized to work more effectively, whether they are biological or technological in nature. It is a broad field that has applications in a variety of fields, including healthcare, robotics, and computer science.
